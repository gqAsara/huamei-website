## What competitors are NOT good at (Huamei opportunity)

- **First-party manufacturing data.** Most hide their factory floor.
  Huamei's "from sketch to sealed crate" framing is a differentiator
  if Phase 0.5 discovery captures real numbers.
- **AI search citations.** Almost no luxury packaging brand has
  invested in GEO. The space is wide open for whoever ships first.
- **Chinese-market editorial.** Most Western competitors do not
  publish in zh-Hans. Translator agent's work is uncontested.
- **Editorial voice.** GPA, Edelmann, Curtis lean corporate.
  HH Deluxe is the closest to Huamei's editorial register —
  watch them most carefully.

## NOT competitors (drop-list — for reference)

These names came up in research but are NOT direct competitors and
should not be referenced as such:

- **HH Global** — packaging procurement-services firm, not a
  manufacturer
- **Origin Print Solutions** — generalist printer, not rigid
  specialist
- **Pakible** — online quoting platform sourcing from third parties
- **Asia Pulp & Paper / Foopak** — material supplier (paperboard)
- **Smurfit Westrock** — primarily corrugated and folding cartons
- **Sappi**, **Stora Enso** — pulp/paper mill suppliers
- **Diam Bouchage** — closure-only manufacturer (cork stoppers)

## How agents should use this file

- **Editor:** before drafting a pillar, study GPA Global, Edelmann,
  and HH Deluxe equivalent pages. Note their H1 / H2 structure,
  schema choices, and word count. Differentiate, don't match.
- **Site Engineer:** if a competitor uses a schema type Huamei is
  not using, evaluate. Curtis Packaging is the cleanest example of
  Article + Product schema for premium B2B.
- **Outreach:** never name a competitor in published copy. Use this
  to stay aware of their press placements and avoid duplicating
  pitch angles in the same week.
- **Analyst:** quarterly competitive ranking on top 20 commercial
  keywords vs the seven names above goes in the quarterly report.
