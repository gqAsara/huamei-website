# Custom perfume box manufacturing: structures, surface treatments, and lead times for fragrance brands

By Sonia Sun, Founder, Huamei 華美 — since 1992. Published 14 May 2026. Updated 14 May 2026.

A perfume bottle is a small, dense, fragile object with high perceived value and a price point that justifies precision packaging. The box is not decoration added to a functional shipping container — it is the first physical experience of the fragrance brand, handled before the cap is removed. Getting the structure right requires matching the box geometry to the bottle footprint, the insert to the bottle weight, and the surface decoration to the brand's visual register. Sonia Sun has run Huamei 華美's four factories in Henan, Zhejiang, Sichuan, and Guizhou since the company's founding in 1992; the sections below describe the structure choices, surface options, and lead-time realities for fragrance packaging.

## What is the standard structure for a custom perfume box?

> A custom perfume box is typically a rigid magnetic-closure box or a telescoping lift-off structure, built from 2.0–2.5 mm greyboard wrapped in 128–350 gsm paper and decorated with hot-foil and emboss. The bottle sits in a moulded insert or a paper-engineered cradle inside. MOQ starts at 200 pieces; samples take 7–10 days.

The two most common structures in fragrance packaging are the magnetic-closure lid-over-base and the telescoping lift-off. The magnetic version presents the bottle through a front-opening reveal; the lift-off version raises the entire lid away from the base in a vertical movement. Both are rigid construction — greyboard core, offset-printed wrap, hand-assembled finish. Both carry the same minimum order of 200 pieces and the same 7–10 day sample turnaround.

A third option — the drawer-and-slipcase — suits limited-edition or gift-set formats where the secondary item (a travel size, a candle, or a small card) shares the box. The slipcase lifts off to reveal a pull-out drawer; the draw of the drawer spaces the reveal over two movements, slowing the opening experience. This is the structure closest to the jewellery box format and it reads as the highest-tier format in the fragrance market.

## Structure selection: what drives the choice for a fragrance brief

The bottle's geometry and the brand's opening-experience brief together determine which structure is appropriate. A flat, square bottle on a wide base suits a magnetic lid-over-base; a tall, narrow bottle suits a telescoping lift-off with a form-fitted insert. A gift set with two or more pieces usually needs a drawer or a multi-compartment tray format.

The insert matters as much as the outer structure. A bottle that moves inside its insert makes noise in transit and communicates cheapness at the opening moment. At Huamei, fragrance inserts are paper-engineered from greyboard and laminated inner paper — no thermoforming — with the cradle depth and width specified to the bottle's exact dimensions from the sample round. The insert holds the bottle within 1–2 mm of tolerance, which eliminates movement.

For the outer box, greyboard at 2.0–2.5 mm is the standard weight class for a single-bottle fragrance box in the 50–200 ml range. Heavier bottles — 200 ml and above, or presentation sets with multiple pieces — move up to 2.5–3.0 mm board for lid rigidity and stack stability.

## Surface decoration for fragrance packaging

A fragrance box's surface decoration is doing two jobs: communicating the brand's aesthetic and surviving the retail environment. A luxury perfume box lives on a shelf, in a display case, or in a drawer, and it is handled more than once — at point of sale, at gifting, at receiving. The decoration has to hold.

Hot-foil on a fragrance box concentrates on the brand mark and any typographic elements on the face of the lid. Huamei stocks seventeen foil colours in-house; the most common choices for fragrance are warm gold, platinum, and matte silver. Foil registration is held to ±0.1 mm — critical when foil is combined with emboss on a fine-line logotype. See [/craft/hot-foil](/craft/hot-foil) for how foil and emboss are coordinated.

Emboss without foil — often called "blind emboss" — is used on fragrance boxes where the brand wants tactile detail without colour contrast. A sculpted logo pressed at 0.6 mm standard depth into a coated art paper reads clearly in raking light; in direct light it is nearly invisible. This is the treatment that signals understated luxury. See [/craft/emboss](/craft/emboss) for die depth and paper-weight interaction.

Soft-touch lamination converts the outer surface from gloss or matte to a micro-velvety finish that changes the tactile register entirely. On a fragrance box, soft-touch on the lid and a gloss-laminated base creates a textural contrast that buyers notice without naming it. See [/craft/soft-touch](/craft/soft-touch) for lamination options.

[FSC](https://fsc.org/en)-certified paper stocks are available across most of Huamei's eighty-paper file, covering the range from uncoated natural papers to coated art papers and specialty cloth wraps.

## Magnetic closure pull-force for fragrance boxes

A magnetic closure on a fragrance box serves two purposes: it holds the lid closed during transport and it creates a satisfying close at the end of the opening. The pull-force has to be calibrated to the lid's weight. A lid that flies open requires a higher pull-force; a lid that requires two hands to open requires a lower one.

Huamei's magnetic closures range from 6 to 50 grams of pull-force at 2,800 Gauss, measured across closure types and board weights. For a fragrance lid in the standard 2.0–2.5 mm greyboard range, the working pull-force is typically 20–35 grams — strong enough to hold during transit, light enough to open with a single motion. See [/craft/magnetic](/craft/magnetic) for pull-force specifications and closure positioning.

## Lead times and MOQ for fragrance packaging

Huamei's MOQ floor for rigid box fragrance packaging is 200 pieces. This applies to both the magnetic-closure and telescoping lift-off formats. The drawer-and-slipcase format carries the same floor; bespoke structural configurations with new tooling may carry a higher floor depending on die-cutting setup cost.

Sample turnaround is 7–10 days from artwork lock — meaning once the die-line, colour references, and foil specifications are confirmed. Production runs complete in 15–20 days from press start. For seasonal fragrance launches (Valentine's, CNY, Christmas), the production schedule should be locked eight to ten weeks before the in-market date to leave room for the sample round, production, and ocean freight.
