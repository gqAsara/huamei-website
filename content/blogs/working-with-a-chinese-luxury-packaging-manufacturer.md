# Working with a Chinese luxury packaging manufacturer

By Sonia Sun, Founder, Huamei 華美 — since 1992. Updated 11 May 2026.

Working with a Chinese packaging manufacturer used to mean a 5,000-piece minimum, a twelve-week wait, and a pallet of inventory in a warehouse you do not own — terms that no longer match how luxury brands and small US founders ship. Across four factories in Henan, Zhejiang, Sichuan and Guizhou, Huamei has shipped luxury rigid boxes to US clients like Serenjoy Box LLC for years; most US-bound work runs out of the Henan press floor. The pages below are the working answer to four questions a US founder asks first: is this real, how long does it take, what does it cost, and how do we talk to each other across twelve time zones.

A note on the source. Most search results for "Chinese luxury packaging manufacturer" are written by trading companies, sourcing agents, or directory aggregators — third parties whose business model is taking a commission on the factory selection. The point of view is necessarily external: *here are factories you could use*. This page is written from inside a factory. Every number below — the 200-piece MOQ, the 7–10 day sample turnaround, the ±0.1 mm registration tolerance — is something Huamei runs on its own press floor and ships under its own name. The angle is the difference.

## What "working with a Chinese manufacturer" actually means today

The 2010s playbook — five-thousand-piece minimums, twelve-week waits, anonymous wholesalers — is no longer how a luxury Chinese packaging factory works with a US brand. The published minimum on rigid construction is 200 pieces; folding cartons go lower. An English-speaking project manager is assigned to every US project. Quality runs against a written ISO 9001:2015 system. Samples are made first, on the chosen stock with the chosen foil and closure, before any production tooling moves. Daily progress photos travel by email and WeChat through the sample stage, and the freight terms most US brands quote against are FOB Shanghai or Ningbo, with the importer of record on the US side handling customs. Huamei keeps ninety-nine structures, seventeen foils, and eighty papers on file across four press floors. The conversation a US founder has with us is mostly the same conversation they would have with a US printer — with a different time zone and a longer freight leg.

## How does working with a Chinese packaging manufacturer work?

> Working with a Chinese packaging manufacturer runs in five phases — brief, sample, production, pre-shipment QC, and ocean freight. From locked artwork to a US warehouse, expect 7–10 weeks for luxury rigid construction. MOQs start at 200 pieces.

The first phase is the brief and quote, usually 1 to 2 business days: artwork files, structure choice, finish list, MOQ, target ship date. The second is the sample — 7 to 10 days from the moment artwork is locked, one physical piece on the chosen stock with the chosen foil and closure, sent by express courier for hand approval or iteration.

Production is the third phase: 15 to 20 days on the press floor, covering the press run, surface decoration, hand-assembly, and line inspection. Pre-shipment QC is the fourth, 1 to 2 days at our gate; a client-side QC firm is welcome to inspect alongside our team. Ocean freight is the fifth and longest leg — roughly 3 to 4 weeks from Shanghai or Ningbo to a US West Coast port, 5 to 6 weeks to the East Coast. Add US drayage and 3PL receiving, and the total from locked artwork to a warehouse pallet is 7 to 10 weeks.

## Quality, certifications, and references

A serious Chinese luxury packaging manufacturer answers three questions on the first call — where the certifications are scanned, what equipment the press floor runs, and which named clients are willing to be referenced. Quality management runs against the [ISO 9001:2015 standard](https://www.iso.org/iso-9001-quality-management.html), the same framework a US procurement lead is used to auditing against. The press floor runs Heidelberg and KBA offset presses. Magnetic pull-force on closure work is calibrated to a 6 to 50 g range at 2,800 Gauss, depending on closure type and board weight. Hot-foil-and-emboss registration is held to ±0.1 mm. All thirteen of our certification scans live publicly at [`/house/certifications`](/house/certifications), so a procurement team can read them before a first call rather than after one. Named work — from Lancôme Paris and Estée Lauder to Wuliangye and Luoyang Dukang — is catalogued at [`/volumes`](/volumes), one project at a time. References travel through that page, not through stock photography.

## Lead times, logistics, and total landed cost

The total time from a locked artwork file to a luxury rigid box on a US warehouse pallet is roughly 7 to 10 weeks; about half is build, half is freight. Samples turn in 7 to 10 days. Production runs in 15 to 20 days. Ocean freight is 3 to 4 weeks Shanghai or Ningbo to a US West Coast port, 5 to 6 weeks to the East Coast. Air freight is available for samples and rush small runs at a meaningful unit-cost premium — useful for a launch event or a press kit, not for a production run. Total landed cost includes the FOB unit price, ocean freight, US drayage, 3PL receiving, and Section 301 tariffs on packaging from China, which the US importer of record handles at customs entry. We model the landed-cost stack at the brief stage so an MSRP can absorb it before sampling begins. Huamei FOBs Shanghai or Ningbo depending on container availability, and quotes against current [ICC Incoterms](https://iccwbo.org/business-solutions/incoterms-rules/). Most US-bound projects ship out of our Henan press floor.

## Communication, IP, and managing the project remotely

A US-based founder and a Chinese factory share about two hours of practical overlap each working day — and the project's pace has to be designed around that, not against it. New York business hours from 7 to 9 a.m. ET line up with Henan business hours from 7 to 9 p.m. China Standard Time, which does not observe daylight savings. During the sample stage, progress photos go out daily by email and WeChat; during production, written status goes out weekly. An NDA is signed on the first call. The artwork stays the client's, exclusive to them; structural prototypes are destroyed or returned at the end of the run on request. An English-speaking project manager is assigned to every US project, and Sonia reads every founder email — see [`/house/people`](/house/people). The structural design files stay on the client's side of the contract. The two-hour overlap is enough to run a launch on, when the schedule respects it.

## Why Henan — the geography of luxury packaging in China

Most discussion of Chinese packaging defaults to the coastal cities — Shenzhen, Yiwu, Yongkang — but the houses building luxury rigid boxes for the global cosmetic and spirits market sit further inland. Henan is on the high-speed rail line connecting Beijing and Guangzhou, and on a multi-day truck route to Shanghai and Ningbo for outbound containers. Operating costs run lower than the Pearl River Delta, which translates into a better unit price for the kind of hand-assembled construction luxury work depends on. Tenure on a Henan press floor tends to run longer than on a coastal one — most of our press operators have more than ten years on the same floor, many longer than twenty. The work passes hand to hand, the way it always has. Three of Huamei's four factories are inland — Henan, Sichuan, Guizhou — with Zhejiang serving the coastal piece. Most US-bound projects ship out of the Henan press floor, where the people who built last year's boxes are the people building this year's.
