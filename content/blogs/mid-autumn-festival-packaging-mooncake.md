# Mid-Autumn Festival packaging: structures, lead times, and formats for mooncake gift sets

By Sonia Sun, Founder, Huamei 華美 — since 1992. Published 14 May 2026.

Mid-Autumn Festival falls in September or October, depending on the lunar calendar. The gift-packaging brief for that date should already be in motion by the time summer arrives. Mooncake sets, tea presentation cases, and baijiu gift boxes for the festival share a brief window between artwork approval and the moment the cartons need to be on a pallet. This page covers the structural formats that work for mooncake and festival gift sets, the decoration options that read well on seasonal packaging, and the calendar that puts a finished production order on the freight dock in time.

## What is the lead time for Mid-Autumn Festival packaging?

> Mid-Autumn Festival packaging from a custom rigid box manufacturer requires 7–10 days for a sample and 15–20 days for a production run. Briefs for mooncake gift sets should be locked by late June for a September festival date, accounting for artwork revision time and ocean freight.

The lead-time calendar works backwards from the in-market date. A September festival date means a distribution centre receiving date in mid-August for domestic delivery, or mid-July for ocean freight to overseas markets. Production order confirmed and on press by late June. Artwork locked and sample approved by mid-June. Brief submitted to the factory by early June. That is roughly four weeks of festival-prep window — tight if the design round includes multiple revisions or if the structure has not been chosen yet.

The structures on file at Huamei — ninety-nine across the rigid, magnetic, drawer, book, and bespoke families — exist precisely to shorten this window. A brand that points to an existing structure from the file needs no structural prototype time; the sample round focuses on decoration, wrap, and foil registration rather than engineering.

## Structure formats for mooncake gift sets

A mooncake set is a multi-SKU problem. The standard festival set carries four or eight mooncakes, sometimes alongside a supplementary item: a tea tin, a small jar, a folded note. The packaging has to hold multiple items without movement in transit, and it has to open in a way the gift-recipient finds ceremonial rather than functional.

Three formats handle this well.

**Drawer-and-slipcase.** A slipcase (wrapped greyboard outer) houses a pull-out drawer (thinner board, lined). The drawer carries a moulded tray insert that holds the mooncakes in position. The reveal happens in two stages — the slipcase is lifted, then the drawer pulled — which spaces out the opening moment and gives the receiver a pause before the product is visible. Used on tea presentation cases such as [Heritage Tea](/volumes/heritage-tea), a structure that translates directly to festival-set work. See [/craft/drawer](/craft/drawer) for structural specs.

**Magnetic clamshell.** A full-face magnetic closure opens to reveal a full tray. The advantage over a drawer is that the complete set is visible at once on opening — useful for an eight-cake arrangement where the pattern of the cakes themselves is part of the presentation. The magnetic pull-force for a lid heavy enough to carry a decorated wrap and closure hardware sits between 25 and 50 grams at 2,800 Gauss. See [/craft/magnetic](/craft/magnetic).

**Telescoping lift-off with internal tray.** The simplest and most schedule-reliable format. Lid lifts off base; a thermoformed or paper-engineered tray holds the cakes. The lid carries all the decoration — foil, emboss, gold-stamped surface — and the base is lined in a contrasting paper. Minimum order floor is 200 pieces; at 200–500 pieces this format has the fewest tooling dependencies and the most predictable production window.

For a gift of eight cakes, the magnetic clamshell reads most ceremonially. For a four-cake retail SKU at volume, the telescoping lift-off is more schedule-reliable.

## Decoration for seasonal packaging

Mid-Autumn packaging carries specific visual conventions: cloud and rabbit motifs, jade-green and gold palettes, red-and-gold combinations for gift editions. Hot-foil stamping in gold or copper is the standard surface treatment for festival rigid boxes, because it reads as celebratory without requiring a digital-print run. Seventeen foil colours are held in-house; gold, rose-gold, and copper are the three most-used on festival SKUs.

Registered emboss-and-foil — where the sculpted die and the foil stamp land on the same letterform — is the highest-detail decoration option on a rigid-box surface. On festival packaging it is used most often for the central medallion motif: a moon, a character, a brand logotype in relief. Emboss depth is typically 0.6 mm on a two-piece magnesium die. For full spec see [/craft/hot-foil](/craft/hot-foil) and [/craft/emboss](/craft/emboss).

Eighty papers are kept on file. For festival sets, the most common choices are a coated art paper at 157 gsm (clean surface for full-coverage foil), a pearlescent stock for a moonlight visual effect, and a duplex paper presenting gold on one face and a satin-touch cream on the reverse. FSC-certified paper stocks are available across most of the file — the [FSC standard](https://fsc.org/en) covers sustainable forestry management for all certified substrates.

## The brief Huamei needs to start a sample

A festival packaging brief does not need a finished design. It needs five things: the structure format, the number of internal positions, the foil colour target, the paper direction, and the production quantity. From those five, a sample can be in hand within ten days of brief approval.

The most time-efficient way to brief a festival set is to point to an existing volume as a structural reference, specify the number of internal positions (four, eight, or mixed SKU), and name the foil finish. The first sample resolves pull-force and foil registration; the second locks the production spec.

For tea and spirits sets — categories where Huamei has the deepest structure file — the brief usually closes in one sample round. See [/volumes/wuliangye-clamshell](/volumes/wuliangye-clamshell) for a spirits gift set in the same product family, and [/industry/spirits](/industry/spirits) for how baijiu and premium spirits packaging decisions differ from cosmetics work.

## Procurement timing for overseas brands

For overseas buyers shipping festival gift sets to North American or European retail, the freight calendar compresses the brief window further. Ocean freight from Henan to the US West Coast runs 25–30 days. Air freight cuts that to 5–7 days but changes the cost shape of the project significantly.

The working rule: brief submission by 1 June for a September Mid-Autumn date. That gives two weeks for sample and revision, four weeks for production, and three weeks for ocean freight — with one week of buffer. A brief arriving in late June can still make a September date on air freight; a brief in July is a next-year conversation unless the volume is high enough to justify priority production and airfreight at full cost.
